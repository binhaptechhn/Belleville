7 Tips to treat bleeding gums at home are less expensive but effective

Bleeding gums is not a rare condition and many people are suffering from it for many different reasons. It is also a whistle signaling that your teeth are having many problems such as gingivitis, abscesses and many other dangerous diseases. But when faced with a natural and often bleeding tooth root, you will feel extremely anxious, not knowing where to start and how to treat it. Just a few minutes reading the article below, you will have more information on how to treat and prevent bleeding gums simply but effectively.

Main Content

  • What is bleeding gums?
  • Reason
  • How to treat bleeding gums at home quickly
  • How to prevent bleeding gums

What is bleeding gums?

Bleeding at the root of the tooth is a condition in which soft tissues such as gums (gum), ligaments, and alveolar bone become congestive and leads to bleeding in areas at the root of the tooth. This condition usually does not cause pain, bleeding is small and can stop on its own after 1-2 minutes. If the tooth root is bleeding with pain, the gums are red and swollen, this is a sign of a more serious disease such as a soft tissue infection, or an abscess, etc.

Reason

Bleeding at the root of the toothcan be due to many different causes.

  • The first is to mention the damage caused by accidental impact when using a brush, toothpick, or floss or by hitting hard objects, when chewing solid, sharp food.
  • Too much tartar causes the gums to become inflamed, red and swollen, which is also a cause of easy bleeding.
  • Using dentures, orthodontic methods are not suitable or are in the process of treatment and restoration.
  • Hormonal disorders and lack of nutrients, especially vitamin C , also cause bleeding gums.
  • Some of the more dangerous and scary causes come from diseases such as diabetes, blood cancer, leukemia and blood clotting disorders.

How to treat bleeding gums at home quickly

Bleeding in the root of the tooth in the mild stage or due to knowing the cause comes from impact factors, you can completely fix it at home.

  • You can use a clean gauze pad soaked in cold water or wrap an ice cube and place it on the bleeding area, holding it tight until the bleeding stops.
  • Using tea bags soaked in cold water and applied to the bleeding root area also helps you to quickly improve this situation.
  • Soaking in diluted warm salt water, or non-alcoholic mouthwash in the morning and evening will help reduce inflammation in the gums, so that bleeding gums will also gradually disappear.
  • Honey and tea are also very good cures for this condition. You can mix a little honey into tea, suck it for 2-3 minutes to relieve pain and disinfect.
  • Some Indian experts have studied and shown that using 1 lemon combined with eating 2 grams of garlic per day will significantly improve.
  • Adding celery, fruits such as mango, guava, orange and grapefruit to the daily menu will form a solid shield to prevent bleeding gums.
  • You can take more drugs such as antibiotics, anti-inflammatory and mineral supplements, calcium, vitamins C, E, K, .... But you need to be careful not to abuse and absolutely follow the directions of the pharmacist.

If the condition does not improve, you need to see a dentist for proper and timely treatment.

How to prevent bleeding gums

“Prevention is better than cure”, this is an important step to help you break up this disease quickly.through the bloodstream, infecting the blood and carrying pathogens that affect your heart.

You should change your oral hygiene habits:use a soft-bristled toothbrush, brush your teeth at least twice in the morning and evening, use dental floss to remove plaque, use an antiseptic mouthwash daily.

Avoid biting and chewing hard and hardfoods. You can break them into small pieces with your hands before putting them in your mouth.

Drink enough water and eat lots of vegetables and fruits to help boost nutrients and vitamin C for the body. As a result, the resistance is increased, against bacteria that cause inflammation and bleeding gums.

Limit drinking alcohol, eating hot spicy foods , because this is the cause of bleeding gums.

You need to get tartar periodically (6 months / time) to destroy the breeding environment of periodontal disease.

With just a few simple steps, you can "discolor" the disease of bleeding teeth and protect your health well.

Bleeding gums is not a rare or dangerous disease, but if you take it lightly and do not know how to treat and prevent it early, bleeding in the roots will cause more serious problems. You need to build a reasonable oral hygiene routine, combine vitamin and mineral supplements to optimize the treatment process.
