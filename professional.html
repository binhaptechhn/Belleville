Dentistry: What to study, where to study and career opportunities


The increasing demand for orthodontics as well as dental care in today's modern life makes dentistry a very popular choice for young people who want to pursue medicine. In this article, Hotcourses Vietnam will introduce to those of you who are interested in medicine in general and have a special interest in the dental industry, complete information about this discipline as well as the future career in this field.


Overview of dental specialties

Dentistry specializes in the study, diagnosis, prevention and treatment of diseases related to teeth and oral cavity such as jawbone, gums, fascia, facial bones and cheeks. Students studying dentistry will be equipped with knowledge about oral hygiene, diseases of dental infections, orthodontic techniques and practice on methods of replacing dentures, restoring teeth, crowns, orthodontics orthodontics. Some of the jobs of a dentist can be mentioned as braces, replacement of dentures, endodontic adjustments such as tooth decay, tartar, tooth extraction. In addition, depending on the specialty, learners need to have a good understanding of how to prescribe drugs and also complex jobs such as surgery, anesthesia, and transplantation.

The dental training majors that students can choose from include:

  • Orthodontic
  • Dental care
  • Dental Restoration
  • Orthodontic orthodontics
  • Orthopedic X-ray of the mouth
  • Dental surgery
  • Pediatric Dentistry
  • Community Health Dentistry

Structure of the dental discipline

If you want to become a professional dentist, the first thing you need to do is study for a bachelor's degree in dentistry. This is the most popular level that dental students choose. The length of study is usually 4 years depending on the school and the field you pursue. During your studies, to gain more hands-on experience you will be able to attend some laboratory classes as part of the course requirements.


After graduating from university, you can work in hospitals and dental clinics. Those who wish to pursue further study in dentistry can take postgraduate courses. Some universities will require 2 to 3 years of experience for you to enter the graduate level. A number of international universities now offer Master of Orthodontic Science or Master of Dental Science courses , which usually last 2-3 years.

Dental career opportunities

These days, in addition to skin and hair, teeth are a factor that many people care about, so dentists are now a "money-making" profession. According to a BBC survey, the income level of doctors operating in the Dental - Jaw - Facial industry is often higher than other professions in the medical field.


Fresh graduates can work in dental clinics, dental clinics, hospitals or non-governmental organizations. When you have accumulated enough experience and want to be more proactive in your work, you can open a private dental clinic by yourself.

Some of the official jobs associated with this discipline are:
  • Dentist
  • Assistant to monitor, take care of teeth
  • Dental Restoration Specialist
  • Dental technician

Interesting things you may not know if you pursue dentistry

Not only learning knowledge about teeth

Although dentistry is primarily concerned with the study of teeth, there are a number of other subjects covered in the course. For example, you will learn the basics of human disease, drug dosing, health psychology and the basics of the physics involved in dental materials. The course covers a variety of topics that can be applied to understanding dentistry and improving patient care.

Right handedness is an advantage

Dentists, dental nurses, and dental hygienists who perform the examination of patients using a dental chair must be right-handed. The reason is that the current domestic dental machine chairs are designed for the right-hand side, most of which are fixed and do not adjust to the opposite side. Although there are dental machines designed specifically for left-handed people in the world, they are not always fully equipped at training institutions or medical facilities, unless it is a clinic. of me. Therefore, if you cannot practice using tools with your right hand, you should consider overcoming them.


Meticulousness and precision are never superfluous

Dental work requires skills in the use of sharp tools and in the patient's oral cavity. It can be seen that the dental assistants / dentists are people who "have the mind of a doctor and the hands of an engineer". Especially with specialized in dental prosthetic techniques, creating dentures is usually quite small in millimeters, porcelain filling, plastic grinding, ... all require certain meticulousness and ingenuity. In addition, when conducting an examination for a patient, the dentist needs to focus to perform correctly to avoid unpredictable consequences. There are problems that should not only be based on experience but also based on the plan, x-ray of the patient's jaw such as extraction of teeth 6,7,8 or wisdom teeth.

Application of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in dentistry

Current dental treatment is not based solely on a thorough examination and analysis of existing patient data. With the development of smart technology, AI has now been introduced into this field with many purposes such as supporting decision making in orthodontics, detecting caries, surveying and diagnosing diseases. periodontitis, even recommending treatment plans. With the support from Artificial Intelligence, the quality of diagnosis and treatment is increasingly improved, in parallel with the level of customer satisfaction.
