What is tooth enamel? 5 effective ways to overcome weak tooth enamel

From the outside, your teeth look healthy. But weak enamel can still make your oral health worse. Strong enamel is one of the most important factors for comprehensive oral care. Therefore, the protection of tooth enamel is very necessary to protect comprehensive oral health.

What is tartar?

Tartar is also known as tartar. These are plaques that have been calcified by Calcium Phosphate – a salt compound found in saliva. Plaque is formed by bacteria that develop after eating and drinking on the surface of the teeth.

If not cleaned properly, these plaques will combine with salt compounds and form tartar. They stick to the surface of teeth or gums as yellow or yellow-brown patches.

harm of tartar

Before deciding whether to get tartar, let's learn about the harmful effects of tartar on oral health! Here are some harmful effects that tartar causes:

  • Tartar is the home of bacteria that are harmful to teeth. These bacteria will ferment carbohydrates to produce acids. From there, the acid will erode tooth enamel and cause tooth decay.
  • Tartar is also the cause of periodontitis, bleeding gums, receding gums. Even in the most severe cases, tartar can cause inflammation and alveolar bone loss. This leads to loose teeth and premature loss of teeth.
  • Tartar is one of the causes of throat diseases such as tonsillitis, mucositis, chronic pharyngitis...
  • Tartar causes teeth to become discolored. It also causes an unpleasant odor on the breath. These things will make you lose confidence in daily communication.

Types of tartar

Normal tartar

These are patches that are pale yellow or milky white. This type of tartar adheres to the surface of the teeth and gums . If not treated in time, tartar will often cause gingivitis.

Serum tartar

It is a more severe symptom of normal tartar. When tartar often causes gingivitis that is not treated in time. From there, gingivitis will cause bleeding at the root of the tooth. This blood will seep into the normal tartar plaques and turn them into a reddish-brown color called serous tartar.

Levels of tartar

  • Grade 1: Tartar has a thickness of less than 1mm, is milky white or light yellow. This is the mildest level when tartar has just formed and is not harmful.
  • Level 2: At this level, tartar will be 1-2 mm thick and dark yellow. With grade 2, tartar will cause mild gingivitis. Symptoms of this disease are light bleeding at the root of the tooth and bad breath.
  • Grade 3: This is the most severe level with tartar thickness up to over 2mm. At this stage, tartar will turn dark yellow brown or dark brown. Tartar will cause severe gingivitis with symptoms such as red swollen gums, bleeding gums and an unpleasant odor in the mouth.

What is tartar scaling?

Scaling is a procedure in dentistry, dentists will use medical tools to remove tartar from the surface of the teeth. Thereby, helping to increase oral health and prevent gingivitis. However, not so that we abuse the tartar. You should get tartar every 6 months.

frequently asked questions about tartar
Question 1: Does tartar peel off on its own or not?

Tartar (calcium) can peel off on its own, which is a misunderstanding of many people. However, in fact tartar is calcified plaque. They stick very firmly to the enamel on the gums. Therefore, it is unlikely that tartar will peel off by itself.

Question 2: Is brushing your teeth enough to clean all the plaque?

The cause of tartar is the calcified plaque caused by the careless oral hygiene process. Brushing alone will not help you remove all the remaining plaque on the surface of your teeth. You need to use more floss and mouthwash to be able to remove plaque most effectively.

Question 3: Can I eat after shaving?

According to advice from dentists, you should not eat and drink immediately after removing tartar. The process of removing tartar will more or less affect the enamel, making the teeth more sensitive. Therefore, you should let your teeth rest for 3-4 hours before eating. At the same time, during 2-3 days after removing tartar, you should avoid foods that are too hot or too cold to avoid causing tooth sensitivity.

Question 4: Can periodic tartar removal stop bad breath?

Tartar is just one of the possible causes of bad breath. Therefore, regular tartar will help you reduce the risk of causing dental diseases. However, if you have done regular tartar, but the odor still does not decrease, you should see your dentist for a checkup!

Question 5:Is tartar removal painful?

Scaling is a common dental procedure . Dentists will use dental tools to remove plaque, tartar on the surface and between teeth. Usually, this is a simple and painless procedure if performed correctly by the dentist.

Question 6:How long does it take to get tartar?

Normally, the procedure to remove tartar will take an average of 15-20 minutes to perform. This period can be increased or decreased depending on whether your teeth have more or less plaque and tartar.

Question 7:How much does it cost to get tartar?

Besides the question of whether to take tartar, the cost of performing tartar is also something that many readers wonder. The cost of taking tartar and polishing (2 jaws) will range from 400,000 to 500,000 VND. For patients with tartar under the gums, the cost will be about 1,000,000 VND.

Scaling teeth with ultrasonic waves

Scaling with ultrasonic waves is the most modern method of removing tartar by machine today. The structure of the tartar removal machine includes: the tartar tool has a small head design with strong pressure, an ultrasonic sensor that helps transmit the signal to the tip of the tool. As a result, the tartar machine has the ability to gently break down the calcified plaque that adheres firmly to the surface of the teeth and under the gums.

Compared with manual methods, the ultrasonic tartar scraping method helps dentists effectively handle tartar. At the same time, this method will be much safer than traditional methods.

Tips to help minimize the formation of tartar

  • Brush teeth after eating for 20-30 minutes, at least twice a day.

If you feel uncomfortable brushing or eating, it's best to make an appointment with your dentist right away. But brushing too hard or brushing with a stiff bristle brush can damage enamel. To keep your enamel healthy, keep these tips in mind:

  • Brush your teeth at least twice a day with a soft-bristled toothbrush.
  • Position your brush at a 45-degree angle when brushing and move the brush briefly and firmly.
  • For a thorough clean, brush your teeth for at least two minutes at a time.
  • Clean all areas of your mouth, including your tongue, to remove the bacteria that normally reside here.

Saliva, secreted after brushing, can also compensate and correct demineralization. Containing minerals and proteins that help protect your enamel from cavities, saliva continuously washes away germs from your teeth even when you're not paying attention.
