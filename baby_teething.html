What do you need to know when your baby is teething?

Teething in babies is always a difficult time for both parents and babies. Therefore, parents need to equip themselves with basic information and knowledge to easily handle when their baby is teething . Usually around the beginning of the 6th month, the baby's first baby teeth will gradually appear, the accompanying "disorders" such as fever, anorexia, etc., always make mothers worried. Let's learn more about this difficult time with Belleville Dental Clinic!

Baby teething signs

The time of teething of baby teeth is not the same, but usually in the period from 6 to 30 months. The main reason is due to:

  • Babies born prematurely, lack of nutrients.
  • Inherited from family.
  • Lack of calcium, vitamin D and other nutrients causes growth retardation and malnutrition.
  • Due to late weaning, gums are not stimulated.
  • Different geographies.

However, parents can guess the time when their baby's baby teeth are coming through signs such as:

  • Expression of crying, fatigue, easily agitated.
  • Excessive drooling, swollen gums with sores.
  • Frequently gnawing objects, fingers.
  • Mild digestive upset.
  • Mild fever, poor diet.

These signs usually appear about 3 to 5 days before the tooth erupts and go away after 3 to 7 days.

Baby teeth eruption process

The order of baby teeth eruption will usually follow the following time periods:

  • From 6 to 9 months: Four central incisors. The lower front teeth will appear first around the 6th month. The child will be irritable, uncomfortable, low-grade fever, .., After the two lower incisors, the two upper incisors will grow in the 8th month.
  • From 7-10 months: Two upper front teeth. 7 months to 10 months, 2 upper front teeth continue to grow, two lower front teeth grow when the baby enters 16 months.
  • From 12-14 months: 4 baby molars. After the incisors grow enough, the molars will gradually appear. The first is the 2 inner teeth of the upper jaw. Then there will be two lower teeth. At this time, it is necessary to focus on supplementing with fluoride and preventing dental disease for children.
  • From 16 to 18 months: 4 milk canines. This tooth will erupt when the child is 16-18 months old. The two lower canines will erupt after the two upper jaws are fully grown. Some babies take up to 22 months to fully develop four milk canines.
  • From 20 to 30 months: The last four primary molars. At the 20th month, the last two molars will fully erupt in the lower jaw. Next will be the appearance of the two upper molars.
Note when your baby is teething

Newborns complete the teething process at the age of 30 months. Parents should keep in mind the order of their children's teething so that they can react promptly when their children have abnormal signs without being too surprised. or bewildered. Monitor your child regularly, remember and mark on the calendar the changes in the child's teeth so that it can be easily discussed with the doctor when needed.

How to take care when your baby is teething

Like adults, when teething babies will experience pain, may have a fever leading to skipping meals, loss of appetite. Therefore, gently take care of the children:

  • Divide meals into 6-8 meals instead of 3-4 as usual. Just eat a little bit at a time.
  • Stew food for children, soft and smooth, preferably in the form of thin porridge, soup. Then it will be easier for the baby because it only needs to be swallowed, not chewed. With newborns, mothers should breastfeed more or express milk and feed the baby.
  • You should squeeze fruit to get water to keep it cool in the refrigerator, to help minimize pain. Because with cool drinks, the gums will be less swollen and painful.
Things to keep in mind when your baby is teething

If your baby has a fever above 38 degrees, take a warm towel and place it on his forehead or wipe his body. Antipyretic drugs can be used after consulting a doctor. Take care of your baby's oral hygiene by wiping his mouth with a soft cloth and wiping his teeth when he's finished eating.

During the teething stage, babies tend to put things in their mouths to chew because their teeth are itchy. Prepare objects made of safe materials for health, soft objects, etc. Be careful that children swallow objects. In case of high fever, prolonged diarrhea, lethargy. You should take your child to the doctor to prevent a bad case. Parents should note the above ways to promptly recognize and improvise when children have similar signs. Just monitor your baby regularly, you will surely understand your baby better and have the most appropriate care.
