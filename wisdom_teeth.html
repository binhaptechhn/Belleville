What are wisdom teeth?

Wisdom teeth are teeth close to the jaw wall, they are called number 8 or molar number 3. Each adult will have 4 wisdom teeth, of which each side will have 2 teeth. Many people mistakenly think that they do not have wisdom teeth, the reason is because the teeth do not grow out of the gums but grow underground in the jawbone.

Most of us will eventually get wisdom teeth, but between the ages of 17 and 25 is the most common age for wisdom teeth to erupt (except for about 20-30% of people who do not have wisdom teeth). This is the last tooth to erupt. When wisdom teeth grow, you will often experience pain due to their compression and misalignment.

What are forbidden teeth?

Forbidden teeth are often called molars or molars, each person will have 8 molars, 4 on each side. It is tooth number 6,7 counting from outside to inside. Its characteristics are that it is very large compared to the front teeth, has a wide chewing surface, so they play an important role in the process of chewing and crushing food.

This tooth usually erupts between the ages of 6 and 13 and will not replace teeth like other teeth. That is why they must be carefully observed and protected. Due to the function of chewing, the food plaque often clings to, so these teeth are very susceptible to decay, once they are damaged, they will lose aesthetics and cause pain for a long time.

Distinguish between wisdom teeth and molars

Distinguish between wisdom teeth and molars with the role of chewing function.

Before food is put into the stomach, it will be chewed and crushed by the teeth to make the digestion process more efficient. However, with wisdom teeth it is different, because they are located too deep (in the back) so they can hardly play the role of chewing food like the molars.

When the doctor ordered tooth extraction

When the molar is damaged or the tooth decay is too severe, the doctor will prescribe extraction. It is the last resort when the teeth can no longer be kept. In other cases, the doctor will prioritize treatment methods to protect the teeth such as: porcelain veneers, dental fillings, etc.

After distinguishing between wisdom teeth and molars, we see that wisdom teeth often grow in adulthood, when all other teeth have grown enough in the jaw, but with a large body, wisdom teeth almost erupt. underground or protruding and adjacent tooth roots. For that reason, wisdom teeth are often an obsession for many people. At this time, the doctor will appoint to extract the tooth to avoid the effects and complications later. On the other hand, when wisdom teeth have grown straight, they have an ugly shape, forming gaps that make food plaque easy to cling to, causing many oral diseases. In such a case, the doctor also recommends extraction to prevent dental disease

What happens when you lose your wisdom teeth or wisdom teeth?

As mentioned, the molars have a very important role because their main function is to chew food. Therefore, unfortunately, you have lost your wisdom teeth, you must immediately find a suitable method of making teeth to replace the denture. If left for a long time, the jaw will appear jaw bone loss, causing receding gums, shifting teeth ...

In contrast to wisdom teeth, wisdom teeth have almost no role, so when lost, you do not need to replant wisdom teeth. Their loss also does not cause jaw bone loss because the tissues inside will fill the gap on its own.

Through the content of the above article, we have distinguished how wisdom teeth and wisdom teeth differ. In short, wisdom teeth are the innermost teeth that will grow in adulthood, they have almost no important function, but on the contrary, when they grow, they also affect your health due to misalignment or underground growth. Completely different from wisdom teeth, molars occupy a very important role, responsible for chewing function, each person's forbidden teeth will have 8 pieces, folk often call them molars.
