What is the boss profit?

The gum is the part of the gum that covers the surface of the tooth. Gums can cover or partially cover a tooth. Normally, when teeth erupt, this part of the gums will gradually disappear. However, in some cases, this gum cover will cover the edema and hinder the development of the teeth. From there, they cause pain for the patient. In the long run, when the teeth come in, push a part of the gum to create a space under the gums. In this case, if you don't take care of your teeth and take care of them carefully, you will most likely face inflamed and swollen gums.

What is gingivitis?

Gingivitis is known to be a form of disease mainly related to the development of wisdom teeth . The gum on the wisdom tooth surface will cause an obstacle that prevents the wisdom tooth from growing in. The wisdom teeth will continue to grow and pierce the gums, causing discomfort for the sufferer. Often in the process of wisdom teeth eruption, you will face this situation many times. With some severe cases of gingivitis, the gums are swollen, causing pain during eating.

Normally, if there is no bacteria attack, the gums will recover on their own after 3-4 days. But in some cases, the inflamed gums can cause lasting pain. They can even give you a fever. In this case, you should visit your dentist for advice and treatment.

Misaligned wisdom teeth are one of the causes of inflammation of the gums that cover the wisdom teeth. Learn about this situation right away through the article: "What are the signs of wisdom teeth that are misaligned? Should wisdom teeth be extracted?

Purulent gingivitis

Purulent gingivitis is a bacterial infection that causes the gums to form a pus-filled capsule that swells the tissues around the crown and root of the tooth. The cause of this condition is that the leftover food is stuck in the inflamed gums. The leftover food becomes an ideal environment for bacteria to nest and enter the gums through the damage caused by the erupting wisdom teeth piercing the gums.

In this case, if not treated promptly, the bacteria will spread through the wound to the surrounding tissue. From there, destroy these tissues, they can even damage teeth and jawbone in the worst case.

Here are some signs to help you distinguish between swollen gums caused by wisdom teeth and purulent gum inflammation:
  • Red swollen gums: The gums covering the wisdom teeth are swollen and red. When pressing on the boss gums, you will feel pain. Drainage and pus may be seen.
  • Toothache: for inflamed gums with pus, you will feel pain in the teeth. The pain will last for a long time, even swallowing saliva or opening the mouth is painful. When the gums are severely inflamed, they will cause inflammation around the crown and jaw
  • Fever and lymphadenopathy: when the gums are inflamed and pus-filled, you may have a fever. The angle of the jaw is swollen and there are lymph nodes in the neck area. These are signs of an infection.
  • Drooling: The gums are inflamed and swollen, making it difficult for you to close your mouth as usual. When you go to sleep, you will see the appearance of saliva flow and an unpleasant odor.

Methods to treat inflamed gums

Depending on the inflammation of the gums, the doctor will give you individual treatment regimens. Here are the methods used to treat gum inflammation. Let's consult Tan Dinh Dental Clinic about the methods!

Treat inflammation with antibiotics

In the case of swollen gums, redness, inflammation, when visiting the doctor, the doctor will examine and disinfect the inflammation. You will then be prescribed antibiotics by your dentist. When the gums become inflamed and pus-filled, the doctor will have to give you antibiotics to kill the bacteria. After the gingiva is more inflamed and stable. Only then can we treat the inflamed gums thoroughly.

Using antibiotics to treat gingivitis is considered a temporary solution to gingivitis. You will usually be prescribed antibiotics for about 5 days. After 5-7 days, the boss benefits will gradually stabilize. However, this is only a temporary solution to help cure gingivitis. Gingivitis may return. When using these antibiotics will partly affect the stomach. So, if you have a history of stomach problems, don't forget to tell your doctor!

Cut boss profit

A gingivectomy is a minor surgery used in dentistry to remove the part of the gum that has grown over the wisdom tooth. In the case of wisdom teeth growing straight, the dentist will perform this procedure. Cutting gums will help free up space and create conditions for wisdom teeth to continue to grow.

When performing a gingival cut, the dentist will clean the oral cavity and numb the part of the gums to be removed. Then, the dentist will use a laser to cut the inside, the outside and remove the root of the gum. Feeling pain, swelling and slight bleeding will be inevitable after cutting the boss gums. Therefore, you should not worry too much about the post-surgery. Usually, after about 1-2 weeks, the gums will completely recover.

Wisdom tooth extraction

Tooth extraction is not known to be the most effective method for dealing with inflamed gums. Wisdom tooth extraction will ensure that gingivitis does not return. Not only definitive treatment of gingivitis. Removing wisdom teeth will help the jaw to have more space. This makes cleaning your teeth easier. Therefore, wisdom tooth extraction is the method recommended by dentists in the treatment of inflamed gums.

Questions about the treatment of purulent wisdom tooth gingivitis

Can gingivitis go away on its own?

In case the gums are inflamed and slightly swollen for wisdom teeth to erupt. At the same time, the inflamed gums are not infected, and the inflammatory gums can completely heal on their own after 3-4 days. However, one thing is for sure, this gum will continue to swell and become inflamed again in the future. The reason is, wisdom teeth will continue to grow. When the gum is partially exposed on the surface of the wisdom tooth, this becomes an easy place for food to get stuck. From there, the bacteria will again cause inflammation to this gum.

Can cutting gums completely solve inflammation?

Minor gum surgery is not always 100% effective. This method will be recommended by dentists in cases where wisdom teeth have grown almost completely. Also, you have no history of gingivitis.

You should remember that once you remove the boss benefits, they still have the ability to grow again. They will continue to have the potential to bring dangerous complications such as: gingivitis, inflammation of the body around the wisdom teeth, inflammation of the pulp ... Therefore, to minimize these complications, the wisdom tooth extraction method is the the most effective method.

Gingivitis what to eat?

When the gums are inflamed, you should avoid some of the following foods to avoid worsening the inflammation of the teeth:

  • Foods that contain a lot of sugar such as cakes, snacks, candies, etc.
  • Limit carbonated drinks: soft drinks, beer…
  • Do not use foods that are too hot or too cold.
  • Hot spicy foods: pepper, chili, mustard…
  • Preparations containing stimulants: coffee, tobacco, alcohol...
