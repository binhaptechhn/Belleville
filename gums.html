Purulent gums: Causes and treatment of purulent roots

Purulent gums are one of the most common oral diseases today. In addition to pain, pus-filled gums in the long run will lead to tooth loss, blood infection, ... extremely dangerous. So if we encounter this unfortunate situation, what should we do?

What is purulent gingivitis?

Gums with pusIt is a condition in which the gums surrounding the tooth socket form a pocket of colored pus white. The density of the white pus pocket on the gums depends on the inflammation the patient has. This is consequences of patients with gum infections that are subjectively not treated early.

Sign ofpurulent gum diseaseusually very obvious and recognizable:

  • When there is purulent gingivitis, the gums will be swollen, dark red or pinkish red.

  • Gums are very sensitive and bleed when brushing, flossing or eating hard foods

  • Appearance of white pus pockets between teeth and gums

  • The patient has worn neck teeth, receding gums, wobbly

  • The mouth has an unpleasant odor due to the decomposition of bacteria

  • In cases where the gums are severely swollen with pus, the patient may have a blood infection, affecting the serious to health.

Causes of gum disease pus

Knowing the cause of swollen gums will help each of us to master Actively monitor their oral health status and promptly prevent dangerous complications. Dental Tan Dinh Department has summarized the main causes of swollen and pus-filled gums as follows:

Patient is suffering from diseases dental

Chipped, traumatized, or decayed teeth can affect the pulp, causing infection. If it's okay If treated early, there will be no accumulation of pus between the teeth and gums, also known as a root abscess.

Due to complications of wisdom teeth

According to statistics, the percentage of patients who come to Tan Dinh Dental Clinic to treat swollen gums pus related complications of wisdom teeth are quite high. If wisdom teeth are misaligned, crossed, hidden or covered Gum filling can also lead to pus formation in the gums.

Is it due to periodontal disease?thoroughly treated

If periodontitis is not treated subjectively, it will progress to the pus-forming gum stage. So if Patients who are suffering from periodontitis should immediately go to a reputable dental clinic to be treated, to avoid complications of purulent discharge gums.

Don't pay too much attention Proper dental hygiene

Improper oral hygiene is one of the leading causes of inflammation The root of the tooth has more white pus. When cleaning teeth, absolutely do not use too hard brushes, toothpicks teeth or brush teeth vigorously because these will contribute to creating conditions for bacteria to enter the affected gum area damage.

>How to treat gums pus

Depending on the stage of disease development, the doctor will offer appropriate treatment solutions, help us quickly get rid of gingivitis:

Treatment of purulent gums stage light

At this stage, the doctor will prescribe some antibiotics against swelling and inflammation in combination with the removal of tartar to remove bacteria that cause gingivitis. Along with that, the patient needs to follow a regimen Scientific oral care as follows:

  • Gargle with salt water or dental mouthwash to disinfect and reduce swelling

  • Use a soft-bristled toothbrush to gently brush your teeth to avoid hurting your gums

  • Eat soft foods, not too hot or too cold

  • Limit sour, spicy, salty foods because they easily irritate the gums.

  • Add foods rich in vitamin A and vitamin C

Treatment of purulent gums serious passage

First, the doctor will proceed to remove tartar hidden deep under the gums. If gum swelling is caused by an impacted wisdom tooth, The doctor will advise removing wisdom teeth combined with anti-inflammatory antibiotics.

For extensive gingivitis, the doctor must surgically remove the damaged periodontal part love. Then, if necessary, the doctor will add an extra gum flap to keep the tooth from moving, leading to risk early tooth loss.
