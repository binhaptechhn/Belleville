Appointment

step-by-step process

Step 1

Customers will be examined by a dentist and have a general dental check-up. Through the examination, the doctor will assess the degree of discoloration of the teeth, the condition of the teeth to see if they are eligible for teeth whitening.

Step 2

The doctor will use the tooth color chart to compare the color of the current teeth and the desired color of the customer's teeth. Normally, the color before and after bleaching is usually about 2-4 color levels difference.

Step 3

After cleaning the teeth, the doctor will wear a lip protector and isolate the gums with a special dental gel. The doctor will apply anti-sensitizing medicine to avoid discomfort when performing and will help protect the teeth.

Step 4

The doctor will personally prepare a whitening agent with a dose and rate suitable for each person's oral condition as well as the desired level of teeth whitening.

Frequently Asked Questions

1.Is teeth whitening painful?

Teeth whitening is usually painless. However, you may experience some cases such as tooth sensitivity, this symptom is normal and occurs in 60% of teeth whitening cases. Depending on the structure of tooth enamel, the degree of sensitivity will be different, your doctor may prescribe pain relievers if you feel discomfort.

2. Taking care of teeth after whitening

Start drinking water at least 3 hours after bleaching.
Two weeks after teeth whitening, you should abstain from using foods and beverages with dark colors such as sauces, coffee, tea, wine... as well as citrus fruits such as oranges, lemons, tangerines...
Do not drink water that is too hot or too cold to avoid tooth sensitivity.
