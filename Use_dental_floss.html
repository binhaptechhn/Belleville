Dental flossis the invention of Levi Spear Parmly - an American dentist in the 20th century 19. After being patented and put into production, the product is recommended by dentists to use widely used.

What is dental floss?

Dental floss (or dental floss)is thin, tough thread, made from nylon or plastic in a sterile environment. In addition, the product is also impregnated with antibiotics, triclosan, fermentation inhibitors, etc. are beneficial for the process cleans teeth and ensures effective sterilization.

Dental floss comes in two types, single and multifilament. With a single strand, it will be tough than, usually coated with smooth wax, penetrates more easily between teeth. On the market of dental floss Usually sold in rolls or in the form of toothpicks, in order to make it easy for users to use and carry around me.

The effect of dental floss

1. Clean food plaque

We often know that Ha Khoa floss is used to clean plaque , food residues clinging to the roots of teeth and gums,small, deep, difficult interdental areas, and at the same time help reverse the symptoms of swollen gums. , yellow teeth and bad odor in the oral cavity.

Especially for those who are using the method of braces with brackets, using dental floss will help you clean your teeth more easily and effectively without shaking or moving the brackets.orthodontic process.

2. Stay away from respiratory diseases

The second use to mention is to help us stay away from respiratory disease . Leftover food stuck in the mouth for a long time is an ideal environment for bacteria that cause respiratory diseases to multiply and develop. From here, the bacterial foci gradually spread, causing sore throat, red mouth sores, and worse, leading to pharyngitis, tonsillitis and bronchitis. Thanks to dental floss, the oral cavity will be kept clean, bacteria no longern have the opportunity to "disturb".

3. Prevention of cardiovascular disease

Another use of floss that will surprise you is the prevention of cardiovascular disease . The area of the teeth and gums contains a lot of nerves, and they transmit to every corner of the body, then focus on the heart. Imagine if you let the nasopharynx become inflamed, infected, these bacteria can completely penetrate through the bloodstream, infecting the blood and carrying pathogens that affect your heart.

Dental floss is also an effective tool to help diabetics avoid some dangerous complications. Patients with diabetes often have weak resistance, so they easily encounter infection problems, which hinder the treatment process and greatly affect their lives. Maintaining oral hygiene will contribute to creating a filter to prevent excess bacteria from entering, causing infection for the patient.

Instructions on how to use dental floss correctly

To fully promote the use of dental floss, you need to know how to use it correctly and strictly follow the following steps:

  • Step 1: Use a toothbrush to clean your teeth after eating.
  • Step 2: Cut a piece of floss from 30-50 cm long, roll it on two index fingers so that there is an excess of 4-6cm and stretch.You will move the thread that goes between the teeth, gently moving from the top down, gently swirling in the root area and pulling the leftover food and plaque out.You alternately release a piece of clean thread from one index finger, and use the other index finger to retract the corresponding thread so that the new thread is always kept tight and clean. You continue to repeat the operation until all the teethare cleaned.
  • Step 3: Use pre-mixed mouthwash or use salt water to clean again. This will help you fight bacteria in your mouth.

Some things to keep in mind when using dental floss

When you first start using dental floss, you should do gentle manipulations , avoid using strong pulling force only between teeth, or pressing hard on the gum wall, this will cause damage to the gums, leading to swelling. and bleeding.

You should not use a piece of floss to clean all between your teeth. Saving in the wrong place will cause bacteria to move from one area to another, causing damage to teeth and gums.

The first time you use it, you may bleed because you are not used to it, you should not be too worried to give up. This is a normal situation because our gums have not yet adapted to this new friend.

Another note when using dental floss is that you need to choose the type that is only suitable for your needs, has quality and has a clear origin to ensure your oral health.

Cheap and convenient is what you usually think of dental floss. Now you can see more uses of this thread in protecting oral health in particular and body health in general.

Dental floss is truly a reliable "companion". With just a small thread, it can help us repel plaque, limit odors, tooth decay, yellow teeth, and bring confidence in communication. Using floss correctly will help you have a beautiful smile, strong teeth and a healthy body.
